Settle Nights

Studies in nocturnal light

1 February 2026 · Yorkshire Dales

There is a particular quality to light after dark in small English towns — the way sodium lamps pool on wet pavements, the glow of a pub sign against limestone, the alien green of a petrol station forecourt.

This collection captures Settle, North Yorkshire, on an ordinary Sunday evening. No special occasion. No event. Just the quiet theatre of a market town after the shops have closed.

Six photographs. Six different qualities of artificial light. One walk through a town of 2,500 souls.

About This Exhibition

These photographs were taken during an evening walk through Settle, North Yorkshire, on Sunday 1st February 2026. The temperature was around 4°C. The streets were wet from earlier rain.

No filters. No post-processing beyond minor exposure adjustment. What you see is what was there.
